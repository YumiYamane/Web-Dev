Recipe #1

  • Time: 30 mins
  • Serves: Vegan Waffles
Ingredients Quantity
milk 300 ml
vanilla bean paste 1 teaspoon
plain flour 275g
baking powder 2 level teaspoons
banana 1 ripe
Greek-style vegan yoghurt to serve
seasonal berries 320g
maple syrup for drizzling

Details

  • Vegan
  • Vegetarian
  • Dairy-free

Step 1:Pour the milk into a blender, along with the vanilla, flour and baking powder. Peel and break in the banana, then blitz until smooth.

Step 2:Preheat your waffle maker. Once hot, ladle in the batter mixture then close the lid, and cook according to the machine instructions, until the waffles are crisp and golden.

Step 3:Transfer the waffles to a plate, top with a dollop of vegan yoghurt, some seasonal berries and a drizzle of maple syrup, to taste.

Source: Jamie Oliver's Vegan Waffles

Recipe #2

  • Time: 46 mins
  • Serves: Air-fryer pineapple tarte Tatin
Ingredients Quantity
flaked almonds 20g
tin of pineapple slices in juice 1 x 425g
stem ginger 1 ball
stem ginger syrup 4 tbsp
sheet of ready-rolled puff pastry ½ x 320g
desiccated coconut 20g
vanilla ice cream 4 scoops

Details

  • Budget-friendly
  • Fruit
  • Pastry
  • Air-fryer
  • Ice cream

Step 1:Remove the shelf from the air-fryer drawer, scatter in the almonds and cook for 4 minutes at 190°C, or until lightly golden, then remove.

Step 2:Drain the pineapple (save the juice for dressings or marinades) and carefully arrange the slices across the base of the drawer, overlapping and cutting to fit as needed. Drizzle over 2 tablespoons of stem ginger syrup, then matchstick and scatter over the stem ginger ball. Cook for 20 minutes at 200°C.

Step 3:Gently stretch or roll out the pastry to the size of the drawer. Use tongs to flip the pineapple, then lay the pastry on top, carefully poking and tucking it in at the edges. Cook for 15 minutes at 200ºC, or until golden and puffed up.

Step 4:Just before the time is up, place the coconut in a shallow bowl and roll each scoop of ice cream in it to coat. Carefully and confidently flip the tarte Tatin out of the drawer on to a board.

Step 5:Drizzle over the remaining 2 tablespoons of stem ginger syrup, scatter over the toasted almonds, cut the tarte into 4, and top each slice with a scoop of coconut-covered ice cream.

Source: Jamie Oliver's Air-fryer pineapple tarte Tatin

Recipe #3

  • Time: 50 mins
  • Serves: Brilliant banana sticky toffee pud
Ingredients Quantity
pitted Medjool dates 200g
unsalted butter 175g
golden syrup 75g
bananas 2 ripe
light brown sugar 150g
large free-range eggs 2
self-raising flour 150g
single cream 75 ml

Details

  • Dessert
  • Banana
  • Cooking with kids
  • Dinner party

Step 1:Preheat the oven to 180°C/350°F/gas 4. Boil the kettle. In a small jug, cover the dates with 150ml of boiling kettle water, making sure they’re submerged.

Step 2:Grease a 20cm x 20cm square baking tin and line with greaseproof paper. Pour in the golden syrup, making sure it covers the base, then dot over 25g of butter.

Step 3:Peel the bananas, slice into rounds and arrange in a single layer over the syrup and butter, then set aside.

Step 4:Pour the dates and their soaking water into a food processor, add 75g each of the butter and sugar, and blitz until combined, then crack in the eggs, add the flour, and blitz again.

Step 5:Gently pour the mixture into the tin over the bananas, then bake for 35 minutes, or until golden and an inserted skewer comes out clean.

Step 6:To make a sauce, put the remaining 75g each of butter and sugar and the cream into a small pan and simmer until slightly thickened and a deep gold in colour, gently swirling the pan occasionally, then pour into a serving jug.

Step 7:Carefully turn out the pud onto a nice board or serving platter and peel away the greaseproof to reveal the sticky bananas. Slice, and serve warm with ice cream and a drizzle of the sauce.

Source: Jamie Oliver's Brilliant banana sticky toffee pud
